About

I am a second year masters student with the College of Information and Computer Science at University of Massachusetts Amherst. I am working as a research student at the Information Extraction and Synthesis Lab at UMass, working with Professor Andrew McCallum on the OpenReview project, where I focus on experimenting with different techniques of learning the expertise of a researcher for the purpose of obtaining automated and high confidence peer-review matches. The research is still on-going but you can get a glimpse of some of my work here. I also worked as an intern with the Research and Development team at Lexalytics, where I focused on obtaining meaningful clusters of phrases in order to group similar phrase segments together, based on their semantic similarity.

My prime areas of interest include machine learning, deep learning and their application to the fields of natural language processing and computer vision. I am also interested in exploring the application of different supervised and unsupervised machine learning techniques to real world arenas like finance and medicine. I have successfully completed several machine learning projects that included aspects of research, programming, data visualization, database management and documentation. I have been the fortunate co-author of several conference papers, two of which have also received the best paper award. More recently, I have been a grader for the graduate level Machine Learning course (CS 589) for Spring 2017, Fall 2017 and Spring 2018.

I always look forward to working on challenging and meaningful machine learning problems that not only stretch the human imagination and creativity but are also applicable in the real world, making everyday lives easier.
