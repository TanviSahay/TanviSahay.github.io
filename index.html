About

I am a second year masters student with the College of Information and Computer Science at University of Massachusetts Amherst. I am working as a research student at the Information Extraction and Synthesis Lab at UMass, working with Professor Andrew McCallum on the OpenReview project, where I focus on experimenting with different techniques of learning the expertise of a researcher for the purpose of obtaining automated and high confidence peer-review matches. I also worked as an intern with the Reseacrh and Development team at Lexalytics, where I focused on obtaining meaningful clusters of phrases in order to group similar phrase segments together, based on their semantic similarity.

I have also been a grader for the graduate level Machine Learning course (CS 589) for Spring 2016 and Fall 2017. I am due to present a poster at the Women in Data Science conference being held in Boston, Massachusetts in January 2018.

My prime areas of interest include machine learning, deep learning and their application to the fields of natural language processing and computer vision. I am also interested in exploring the application of different supervised and unsupervised machine learning techniques to real world arenas like finance and medicine. I have successfully completed several machine learning projects that included aspects of research, programming, data visualization, database management and documentation. I have been the fortunate co-authors of several conference papers, two of which have also received the best paper award.
